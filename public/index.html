About Me

I'm a front-end software engineer with years of experience designing and developing applications for the internet. Right now, I'm studying data science and user experience design as an undergraduate student at the University of Toronto Faculty of Information. I'm also working as a UI/UX design research fellow investigating dark patterns – the marketing and design strategies that manipulate user decisions. My educational interests focus on the intersection of technology, ethics, and privacy. I previously presented my research on mass surveillance at the Digital Humanities Summer Institute's conference, and I'm in the process of publishing my first peer-reviewed article on surveillance.

Outside of academia, I previously managed Icarus Labs, a web development and design agency that focused on helping small businesses and charities build their online brands. Under my leadership, the company grew to support over a dozen clients' website, design, and marketing needs. I also worked as an intern analyst for Churchill Capital, a boutique financial firm that taught me how to analyze patterns and predict trends in large data sets.
