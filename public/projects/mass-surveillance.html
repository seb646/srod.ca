Challenge

The field of surveillance studies is a broad aggregation of interests in privacy, technology, law & democracy, economics, and communication. While significant work has been done by scholars to inform the academic community on mass surveillance, the concepts are often challenging for the general public to understand due to the complexity of government systems and policy.

Solution

Translate and deconstruct the complex legal framework supporting the surveillance state into language the general public can understand. Develop a website and write a paper that provides people with information that can be used to reassert their rights to privacy and concent, as well as resist further erosion of democratic governance.

Impact

  • Published a research paper, titled The United States of Surveillance: An Analysis of America's Mass Surveillance Laws, Programs, and Oversight, in the Canadian Social Knowledge Institute (C-SKI)'s IDEAH journal.
  • Developed an open-source website allowing other scholars, experts, and activists contribute to the growing documentation of surveillance laws and programs in the United States.
  • Presented the website and research at the Digital Humanities Summer Institute (DHSI)'s 2021 Conference & Colloquium.
